Objective

The objective of this project was to analyze loan application data and identify the factors that influence loan approval decisions.

Key Findings
1. Credit History is the Most Important Factor ⭐
Applicants with good credit history had an approval rate of 79.05%.
Applicants with poor credit history had an approval rate of only 7.87%.

Conclusion:
Credit History is the strongest predictor of loan approval.

2. Applicant Income and Loan Amount are Related
Correlation = 0.57

Conclusion:
Applicants with higher incomes generally request larger loans.

3. Gender has Minimal Impact
Male Approval Rate = 69.12%
Female Approval Rate = 66.96%

Conclusion:
Gender does not significantly influence loan approval.

4. Self-Employment has Minimal Impact
Self-employed Approval Rate = 68.29%
Non-self-employed Approval Rate = 68.80%

Conclusion:
Employment type has little effect on loan approval.

5. Loan Amount Alone is Not a Strong Predictor
Average Loan Amounts for approved and rejected applicants were very similar.

Conclusion:
Loan amount alone does not determine approval.

Business Recommendations
Recommendation 1

Focus more on:

Credit History
because it is the strongest indicator of repayment behavior.

Recommendation 2
Develop special programs for applicants with poor credit history to improve their eligibility.

Recommendation 3
Use multiple factors together instead of relying only on income or loan amount.
