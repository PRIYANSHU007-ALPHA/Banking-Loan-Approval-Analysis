Q: Why did you analyze Marital Status?
Answer:I analyzed marital status to determine whether being married influences loan approval decisions. Comparing approval rates helps identify whether marital stability is associated with lower credit risk.

Q: Why did you analyze Education vs Loan Status?
Answer:I analyzed education level to determine whether educational qualifications influence loan approval decisions. Comparing approval rates helps identify whether graduates are perceived as lower-risk borrowers.

Q:Why did you analyze Self Employment?
Answer:I analyzed self-employment status to determine whether employment type affects loan approval. The approval rates were nearly identical, indicating that self-employment is not a major factor in loan approval decisions.

q:Why did you analyze Applicant Income?
Answer:I analyzed applicant income because income is a key indicator of financial stability and repayment capacity. Comparing average incomes between approved and rejected applicants helps determine whether income influences loan approval decisions.

Q:Why did you analyze Loan Amount?
Answer:Loan amount directly affects lending risk. Higher loan amounts increase the bank's exposure, so analyzing loan size helps determine whether larger loans face stricter approval criteria.

Q: Why did you analyze Credit History?
Answer:Credit history is one of the most important indicators of repayment behavior. By comparing approval rates, I wanted to determine whether past repayment performance influences loan approval decisions.

Q: What was the most important finding in your project?
Answer:Credit History was the strongest predictor of loan approval. Applicants with good credit history had an approval rate of 79.05%, while applicants with poor credit history had an approval rate of only 7.87%.

Q: What did you learn from the correlation analysis?
Answer:The correlation analysis revealed that Applicant Income and Loan Amount have a moderate positive relationship (0.57), meaning higher-income applicants tend to request larger loans. Most other numerical variables showed weak correlations, indicating limited relationships between them.
